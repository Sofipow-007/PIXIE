# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Makers and enthusiasts who built or want to build the PIXIE hardware (ESP32 + OLED). Also digital-only users who want a software companion on their desktop. Age range 16-35. They're already in their workspace when they open this — monitor lit, coffee on desk, headphones on. The interface sits in a side panel or pinned tab. They glance at it, not study it.

## Product Purpose

PIXIE is a pocket-sized desk companion — a physical ESP32 device with an OLED face that shows the time, weather, and little expressions. This web app is its brain: onboarding the device, configuring its five modules (standby face, clock, weather, timer, translator), and running a software-only "digital mode" for people without hardware. Success means the device feels alive and cared for. Failure means it feels like a settings panel.

## Brand Personality

Alive, precise, warm-weird. Like a well-made piece of hardware you find yourself talking to.

## Anti-references

- Tamagotchi apps that look like toy UI (Comic Sans, rainbow buttons, bubble everything)
- Corporate IoT dashboards (gray, cold, enterprise-boring)
- Generic SaaS dark mode (navy + white, no personality)
- Figma-clone color systems (purple/indigo as default "tech" color)

## Design Principles

1. **The device is the star.** Every screen either talks about what PIXIE does or helps it do its job better. Never generic.
2. **Compact and dense when working, open when welcoming.** The dashboard and settings earn density. The onboarding earns space and breath.
3. **Warmth from the accent, not the surface.** Background stays near-black (OLED native). All warmth comes from the brand color bleeding at edges and fills.
4. **Familiar affordances, unusual flavor.** Standard form controls and layout patterns — nothing reinvented — but the color system and micro-details make it distinctly PIXIE.
5. **Motion only when the device itself would move.** Face animations, sync pulses, module activations. Not page choreography.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion respected for all transitions. All interactive elements keyboard-accessible with visible focus rings. No color as sole state indicator.
